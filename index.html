Camping in Legal

Legal has a seasonal campground. An RV park with the campground locates on the east side of town. The park has playground equipment and a fishing pond adjacent to the park, allowing campers to enjoy fishing while camping. The park is also within walking distance of a grocery store, pharmacy and cafes. Camping is available between April 26 and October 15. The campground may also be open during November if there is no snow. Please get in touch with the City Center for details.

What you should know about Legal?

Best place to have a lunch

Legal has three restaurants. Bigmouth McGee's is one of the most popular restaurants. You will be impressed by the small-town, homey flavour, and The Illegal Burger and fries combo is the most popular menu item. Inside the restaurant, you can enjoy playing billiards and karaoke. In addition, the restaurant also serves alcoholic beverages so that you can enjoy alcohol and beer at lunchtime on your days off.

Painting History in Legal

Legal is known as the French Mural Capital of Canada, with more than 30 murals on display throughout the town. Often the paintings are on different walls of the same building. First-time visitors will quickly realize that these murals are not mere decorations but represent the town's history and region. These murals were painted in 1997 with the help of ACFA. Twelve artists were invited to Legal to depict the history of French immigration in the area. Moreover, Legal recognize as the French Mural Capital of the World in 2011.

Sports activity

There are many sports activities in Legal, such as volleyball, yoga, and floor hockey. One of the most popular activities is pickleball, played by more than 4 million people worldwide. Pickleball is played with a plastic ball across a center net on a court about the same size as a badminton court. This event will be held every Monday and Thursday from 7:00 to 8:30 pm until mid-December. Admission to this event is free for ages five and under and 60+ and $5 for all others.

Movie night

Legal has a movie night every Saturday at 6:00 pm, and you can enjoy a movie in the theatre room of the Community Center. Films are shown from the oldest to the newest and are determined by a weekly drawing. Movie night is free for ages five and under and 60 and over. All others may attend for $5.

Christmas Event

Legal holds a Christmas event every year. It is held at a local community center where participants can enjoy board games and food with the community members. Admission is free for those 18 and under, and tickets are $10 for those older than 18. We have not held the event until last year due to covid-19, but this year we will hold a Christmas event for the first time in two years.

Want to add a new routine to your day?

This class is an event that will introduce new workouts to participants while maintaining a social distance. We have a different workout planned each week! Keep your health fresh in the winter when you are less active. We will incorporate elements of our popular barre classes, including strength, muscular endurance, cardio, balance, core work, and more. Have you ever been ice fishing in Alberta?

Legal will be a great place to go ice fishing in the winter, and Legal Lake is open for ice fishing around January. For those new to the sport, we have equipment available for rent, so please feel free to visit us at the City Center. A complete set of equipment is $50 for one day of unlimited use. We recommend bringing a warm down jacket.

Join us at the Second Hand Sale to get cheap household goods!

Every six months, Legal holds a second-hand sale. This year it will be held on November 30 at the RV Park, where residents will sell furniture and household items they no longer need. This event is very popular, and many people from outside the city of Legal attend every year. You do not have to be a local to sell, but you do need to register in advance to be allowed to sell. Please note that there will be no heaters at the venue on the day of the event, so please dress warmly.

Take a town tour and enjoy all that Legal has to offer!

Join us daily from 11 am-2 pm for a town tour of Legal's famous murals, local restaurants, and more. Local people will be happy to tell you more about the murals and their history. Reservations are required, and we recommend you check with the Community Center at least three days in advance.

New rules for generator hours of use in Alberta's provincial parks.
Camping in Legal

Thanks to a measure by Alberta Parks, campers who want to spend some quiet time alone outdoors can now do so without being disturbed by an intrusive generator. Campgrounds in Alberta have become popular, and many campers are now visiting Alberta. This has caused many problems with noise and other disturbances. In response, starting June 1, Alberta will implement a pilot program to restrict the use of generators at certain campgrounds during certain hours of the day. At campgrounds, generator use will be restricted between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 11:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. and 7:00 p.m.
The following campgrounds will participate in this pilot program Bow Valley and Bow River at Bow Valley State Park, Lodgepole and Ferguson Hill at Cypress Hills State Park, Gooseberry at Gooseberry State Recreation Area, Long Lake at Long Lake State Park, Micron Lake at Micron Lake State Park, Peter Lougheed State Park Elkwood, Pigeon Lake, Zeiner, and Wabaman Lake campgrounds.
According to the Province of Alberta, the goal is to minimize noise while continuing to employ generators. Campers who need extra electricity are encouraged to reserve sites with power. Exceptions are made for using generators for medical reasons, and other arrangements may be made. Since this is an entirely new program, if campers change or cancel their campsites before April 22, the state will refund the $10 change or cancellation fee. Only campsites participating in the pilot program will be refunded. Alberta hopes that the new rules will allow more campers to enjoy camping. The addition of this new rule does not affect fuel cells. Fuel cells may be used as often as desired. However, at least one campground user must be present while the fuel cell is in use. This rule is intended to prevent wildfires caused by fuel cells.
